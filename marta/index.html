Research Interests

For my official web page at the university of Sheffield click here

At moment I live in Sheffield, U.K. where I am pursuing my scientific interests in Computational Biology. I am currently a Wellcome Trust Research Fellow in an Advanced Training scheme. My research  focuses on both biological and computational aspects of mammalian inner ear data, working jointly in the Department of Biomedical Science and Computer Science at the University of Sheffield. Based on previous results obtained by analysing Affymetrix gene array data with probabilistic models, I am building new framework to explore a regulatory network that describes the link between Insulin-like Growth Factors (IGFs) and the gata-3 transcription factor in the mammalian inner ear.  In my previous projects at University of Sheffield at the Department of Biomedical Science and Computer Science, Machine Learning Group, I  mainly worked on the analysis and inference of Affymetrix GeneChip and spotted cDNA gene expression data. I am involved in the PUMA project (PUMA: Propagating Uncertainty in Microarray Analysis) in collaboration with the School of Computer Science at the University of Manchester in UK. In my previous post at the department of  Computer Science, I also focused on feature selection in classification of gene expression data and reducing the image processing variability in cDNA micro-array measurements.

I graduated for my Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics and Computer Science at the University of Naples in April 2000, with my thesis Aspects of Sequence Learning in Artificial Neural Networks. I was supervised by Professor F. E. Lauria in the Department of Physics at the University of Naples, Italy. My work centred upon neural networks applied to dynamic systems. In particular it was focused on problems of sequence learning, involving the concept of implicit learning and knowledge categorization. Despite the complex nature of these problems, our application was based on very simple learning procedure. We implemented such learning paradigms through Hebbian learning algorithms used together with  Boolean neural networks.

In 1998 I was able to take advantage of an opportunity to spend six months at the SVR Laboratory in the Engineering Department, University of Cambridge, U.K.. During my visit there I worked with Professor Mahesan Niranjan, who is now at Sheffield University. My research at the Engineering Department was based on using support vector machines for solving problem of learning to model time series data. Therefore my thesis investigated sequence learning problems for continuous learning and for inference learning, presenting solutions for both aspect of sequence learning problems.

Brief Biography

I studied Mathematics at the University of Naples and I graduated first class with honour in March 1994. I worked for Olivetti Ricerca (Olivetti Research) for 18 months and then I started my four years Ph. D. programme in Applied Mathematics and Computer Science at the University of Naples. 

Personally, I enjoy classical music and I love the violin. I enjoy spending time with my husband, Neil, and my little boy, Federico. Neil and I are also Advanced Open Water Divers, we liked diving in the Red Sea, where we went for our honeymoon. 

As a truly Neapolitan girl, I love eating pizza and seafood in front of the wonderful scenario of the bay of Naples.  And after a good meal, as always...... na' tazzulell' e' cafe' (a nice espresso).
